Loaf #4 back in Boston.  After making ~ a dozen loaves in Colorado following Artisan bread in 5 minutes a day, I incorporated some techniques from the The Breadmakers Apprentice.

  • Std. Boule recipe from ‘5 minutes’
  • 35 mins @ 425 degrees

Preparation Notes: I’ve been using Active Dry yeast, which I’ve read needs time to hydrate before incorporation.  So this time I let the yeast + water soak for 20 minutes and then slowly added flour and salt. I kneaded the dough by stand mixer for a couple minutes until the ingredients were dispersed and the dough turned slightly elastic, then let rise for about 2 hours. I then de-gassed the dough (punch down) several times and let rise again for another ~2 hours.  From here, the dough sat in the refrigerator for 2 days.

Result: Lots of rise! The holes within the bread (crumb) are more evenly distributed then earlier attempts, but the crust is a bit spongy.  I prefer a crispier crust. Maybe caused by the punch down?
